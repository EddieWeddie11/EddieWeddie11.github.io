What is Emotional Intelligence?

  • Emotional intelligence (EI) is the ability to understand, use, and manage your own emotions in positive ways to relieve stress, communicate effectively, empathize with others, overcome challenges, and defuse conflict. It involves: Self-awareness: Recognizing your own emotions and how they affect your thoughts and behavior. Self-regulation: Controlling or redirecting your disruptive emotions and impulses, and adapting to changing circumstances. Empathy: Understanding the emotions of others and treating them according to their emotional reactions. High emotional intelligence helps individuals navigate social complexities, lead and motivate others, and succeed in both personal and professional life.

    • How is EQ different to IQ?
  • Emotional intelligence (EQ) and intelligence quotient (IQ) differ primarily in focus and application. EQ involves understanding and managing one's own and others' emotions, encompassing self-awareness, empathy, and social skills, and it greatly influences personal relationships and leadership abilities. In contrast, IQ measures cognitive abilities such as logical reasoning, problem-solving, and information processing, impacting academic performance and technical skills. While EQ can be developed and enhanced over time through practice, IQ is relatively stable and innate, often assessed through standardized tests. Together, EQ and IQ contribute to a person's overall success, but they highlight different aspects of human intelligence.

    • Why is EQ important?

  • Emotional intelligence (EQ) is important because it helps you understand and manage your own emotions, connect with others, handle stress, and make better decisions. High EQ improves your relationships, communication, and ability to work well with others, making it essential for personal and professional success
